<p><p>The food emulsifiers market encompasses various types that enhance product stability and texture. Di-glycerides, derived from glycerol and fatty acids, improve moisture retention and consistency in baked goods. Lecithin, often sourced from soy or egg, acts as a natural emulsifier, promoting a smooth blend in dressings and chocolates. Sorbitan esters enhance emulsification and are popular in margarine and bakery products. Stearoyl lactylates improve dough quality and volume, while polyglycerol esters serve as versatile emulsifiers in a range of applications, including dairy and confectionery.</p></p>
