<p><p>The microwave ceramic capacitors market is divided into two main types: Class 1 and Class 2 ceramic capacitors. Class 1 capacitors are characterized by their stable capacitance over varying temperatures and voltages, making them ideal for precise applications in communication systems and RF circuits. In contrast, Class 2 capacitors offer higher capacitance values and are less stable but are commonly used in applications that require compact size and cost-effectiveness, such as consumer electronics and automotive systems.</p></p>
