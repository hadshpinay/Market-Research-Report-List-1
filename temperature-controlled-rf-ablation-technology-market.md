<p><p>Temperature-controlled RF ablation technology encompasses various methods for targeted tissue destruction. Ultrasound ablation uses high-frequency sound waves to heat and destroy tissue. Laser ablation employs focused light energy for precise tissue removal. Electrical ablation utilizes radiofrequency energy to generate heat, effectively targeting specific areas. Cryotherapy entails freezing tissue, leading to cell death. Microwave ablation employs electromagnetic waves to generate heat for tissue disruption, while hydrothermal ablation uses heated fluid to achieve similar results. Each method serves unique clinical applications.</p></p>
