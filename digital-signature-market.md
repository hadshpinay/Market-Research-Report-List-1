<p><p>A digital signature is a cryptographic mechanism that provides authentication and integrity for digital messages or documents. It uses a pair of keys: a private key that signs the document and a public key that verifies the signature, ensuring that the document has not been altered in transit and confirming the identity of the sender. This technology is widely utilized in various sectors, including finance, healthcare, and government, to facilitate secure electronic transactions and streamline workflows.</p><p>The Digital Signature Market is poised for significant growth, driven by the increasing demand for secure online transactions and the adoption of e-governance initiatives. <p><p>The digital signature market consists of two main types: hardware and software. Hardware digital signatures involve physical devices, such as smart cards or USB tokens, that securely store cryptographic keys, enhancing security during the signature process. Both types cater to various industries, ensuring document integrity and authenticity while complying with legal and regulatory standards, thus driving their adoption globally.</p></p>
