<p><p>The multi-channel audio codecs market consists of two primary types: analog and digital. Analog audio codecs process audio signals in their continuous form, typically used in traditional audio systems and older technologies. In contrast, digital audio codecs convert audio signals into binary data, facilitating advanced compression, streaming, and higher sound quality. Digital codecs dominate modern applications, including multimedia consumption, gaming, and broadcasting, due to their efficiency and ability to support complex multi-channel formats, enhancing the overall listening experience.</p></p>
