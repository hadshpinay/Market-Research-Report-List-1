<p><ul><li>Pumped Hydro Storage (PHS)</li><li>Compressed Air Energy Storage (CAES)</li><li>Others</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Mechanical energy storage encompasses various methods for storing energy for later use. Pumped Hydro Storage (PHS) utilizes water elevation changes, pumping it to a higher reservoir during low energy demand and releasing it to generate electricity when needed. Compressed Air Energy Storage (CAES) compresses air in underground caverns, releasing it to drive turbines during peak demand. Other methods include flywheels, which store energy in rotational motion, and gravitational energy storage, which employs elevated masses, providing flexible, reliable energy storage solutions.</p></p>
