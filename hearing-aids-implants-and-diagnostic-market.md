<p><p>Hearing aids are categorized into analog, digital, and disposable types. Analog hearing aids amplify sound in a linear fashion, while digital hearing aids convert sound into digital signals for clearer processing. Disposable hearing aids offer a temporary solution for hearing loss. Hearing implants like cochlear implants bypass damaged parts of the ear and stimulate the auditory nerve, while bone-anchored hearing aids (BAHA) transmit sound through bone conduction. Diagnostic tools like audiometers and otoscopes assess hearing health and ear conditions, respectively.</p></p>
