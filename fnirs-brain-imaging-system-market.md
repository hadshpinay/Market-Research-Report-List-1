<p><p>The fNIRS (functional Near-Infrared Spectroscopy) Brain Imaging System is a non-invasive neuroimaging technology used to measure brain activity by assessing changes in blood oxygenation levels. It operates on the principle that oxygen-rich and oxygen-poor blood absorb near-infrared light differently, allowing researchers and clinicians to infer neural activity based on hemodynamic responses. This technology is gaining traction in various fields, including clinical diagnostics, cognitive neuroscience, and brain-computer interfaces, owing to its portability, safety, and ease of use.</p><p>The fNIRS Brain Imaging System Market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 12.3% during the forecast period, driven by increasing demand for advanced neuroscience tools and growing research funding in brain-related studies. The rising prevalence of neurological disorders is also propelling market expansion. Recent trends indicate a surge in the integration of fNIRS with other modalities, such as EEG and MRI, to enhance data accuracy and depth of analysis. Moreover, applications in real-time monitoring of brain function during surgeries and in educational settings for cognitive assessments are emerging, further supporting market growth. The focus on developing wireless and compact systems is expected to drive innovation and adoption in the coming years.</p></p>
